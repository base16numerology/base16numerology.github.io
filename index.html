The Process

How Base 16 Numerology Works

A computable, transparent, open-source system. No hidden formulas. No vague platitudes. Just bytes, hashes, and honest interpretation.

01

Your Data Becomes Bytes

Your name is encoded as Unicode bytes (UTF-8). Your birth moment is converted to a Unix timestamp — the number of seconds since January 1, 1970. Every character, every second, matters.

02

LIBRE-256 Hashes the Bytes

Those bytes pass through LIBRE-256, a small, open-source hash function that compresses any input into a single byte (0–255). It's deterministic, inspectable, and freely available.

03

Modulo 16 Reveals Your Digit

The byte is reduced modulo 16, yielding a single hex digit: 0 through f. This digit is your archetype for that input — one of sixteen fundamental patterns.

04

I Interpret the Pattern

Your five core digits are arranged into a Hex Soul Map. I read the relationships between them — the harmonies, the tensions, the narrative arc — and translate it into practical guidance for your life.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this like regular numerology?

Base 16 Numerology shares the spirit of traditional numerology — using numbers as a mirror for self-reflection — but the mechanics are completely different. We use hexadecimal (base 16) instead of decimal (base 10), Unicode bytes instead of A=1/B=2 tables, Unix timestamps instead of calendar dates, and a hash function instead of digit-summing. The result is a system that works natively with any language, any script, and any level of time precision.

Do I need to understand computers or hex to benefit from a reading?

Not at all. The technical details are there for transparency — so you know the system is consistent and inspectable — but you don't need to understand them to benefit from the archetypes and guidance. Most of my clients have no technical background. The reading is delivered in plain language.

How is this different from astrology or tarot?

Astrology uses planetary positions, tarot uses card imagery, and Base 16 Numerology uses hexadecimal computation. The key difference is transparency: you can see exactly how your result was computed, verify it yourself, and even fork the algorithm. There's no hidden mechanism — just bytes, hashes, and interpretation.

What information do you need from me?

For a Hex Soul Map reading: your full birth name (as it appears on your birth certificate), your birth date, your birth time (as precise as possible), and your birthplace. For relationship overlays, I need the same information for both people.

What if I don't know my exact birth time?

I can work with approximate birth times. If you have no birth time at all, I can compute your Life Path using noon on your birthday as a reasonable default — though I'll note this approximation in your reading. Your name-based numbers (Expression, Soul Urge, Personality) don't require birth time at all.

Does this work for non-English names?

Yes. This is one of Base 16 Numerology's key advantages. Because we work at the Unicode byte level, names in Chinese, Arabic, Hindi, Russian, Korean, Japanese, Hebrew, and any other script are processed directly — no transliteration needed. If a computer can encode your name, the system can read it.

Is this a science?

No, and I never claim it is. Base 16 Numerology is a structured self-reflection framework built on real computing concepts. The computation is deterministic and verifiable, but the interpretation is symbolic. Think of it as a rigorous tool for self-exploration, not a scientific measurement of personality.

How are readings delivered?

All readings include a video consultation (via Zoom or Google Meet) and a written PDF report. The consultation lets us discuss your map in real-time and explore the areas most relevant to your current situation. The PDF serves as a reference you can return to over time.
